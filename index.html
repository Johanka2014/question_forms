Subject and Object Questions

Welcome to this lesson on subject and object questions. This guide will help B1 learners master these essential question forms through clear explanations and practical exercises.

by Joanna McKeogh

What Are Subject and Object Questions?

Definition

Subject questions ask about who or what performs an action.

Object questions inquire about who or what receives the action.

Both types are essential for natural communication.

How to Form Subject Questions

Simple Structure

Question word + main verb

No auxiliary verbs (do/does/did) required!

  • Who lives here?
  • What happened yesterday?
  • Which student passed the exam?

How to Form Object Questions

More Complex Structure

Question word + auxiliary + subject + main verb

Requires do/does/did (except with be/have/modals).

  • Who did you see?
  • What has David lost?
  • Where do they live?

Subject vs. Object Question Comparison

Subject Questions Object Questions
Who visited the museum? Who did Anna visit?
What happened yesterday? What did you do yesterday?
Which book fell down? Which book did you choose?
No auxiliary needed Auxiliary required

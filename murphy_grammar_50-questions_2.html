Indirect & Reported Questions

Indirect Questions

When a question is part of a longer sentence (starting with phrases like "Do you know...", "Could you tell me...", etc.), the word order changes to a normal statement structure (subject + verb).

Direct: Where has Tom gone?

Indirect: Do you know where Tom has gone?

With do/does/did:

We remove `do/does/did` and change the verb form if necessary.

Direct: What time does the film start?

Indirect: Do you know what time the film starts?

With yes/no questions:

We introduce the question with if or whether.

Direct: Did anybody see you?

Indirect: Do you know if/whether anybody saw you?

Reported Questions

When we report what someone asked, we use the same indirect question word order. The verb tense often shifts to the past (e.g., 'are' becomes 'were').

Direct: The officer said, "Where are you going?"

Reported: The officer asked where we were going.

Direct: Clare said, "What time do the banks close?"

Reported: Clare wanted to know what time the banks closed.

Forming Questions

Basic Word Order

In questions, we usually put the subject after the first verb.

Statement: Tom will be here. → Question: Will Tom be here?

Statement: The house was built. → Question: When was the house built?

Using 'do/does/did'

For simple present and past tenses (when there is no other auxiliary verb), we add do/does or did.

Present: You live near here. → Do you live near here?

Past: You sold your car. → Did you sell your car?

When 'Who/What' is the Subject

Do not use do/does/did if the question word (Who, What, Which) is the subject of the sentence. The word order is the same as a statement.

Object Question: Who did Emma phone? (Emma is the subject)

Subject Question: Who phoned Emma? (Who is the subject)

Example: Which bus goes to the centre? (not: Which bus does go...)

Negative Questions

We use negative questions (e.g., Isn't it...? / Didn't you...?) to show surprise or when we expect the listener to agree.

Surprise: Didn't you hear the doorbell? I rang it three times.

Expecting agreement: Haven't we met before? Yes, I think we have.

Note the word order with 'Why':

Why don't we eat out? (not: Why we don't...)
